Physical Design Optimization

In the field of Electronic Design Automation (EDA) for Very-Large-Scale Integration (VLSI), physical design optimization focuses on power consumption, circuit performance, and chip area (PPA). EDA tools automate complex tasks in the design process, such as circuit simulation, layout design, and verification, significantly enhancing efficiency and accuracy. Advanced silicon-scaling technologies (3D IC, FinFET, GAA etc.) further increase design complexity at the SoC level.

Our lab researches EDA methodologies and AI-driven techniques (machine learning & reinforcement learning) covering the full flow of physical design, analog circuit optimization, standard-cell generation, and design-technology co-optimization (DTCO). We also participate annually in the ISPD & ICCAD contests. Current research topics:

  • Physical Design
  • Analog Circuit Optimization
  • Design-Technology Co-Optimization
  • Standard Cell Generation
  • ML-based Prediction & Optimization
  • ML-based Generation
  • RL-based Optimization
  • Design Space Optimization

Deep Learning Hardware

Deep Neural Networks (DNNs) outperform conventional algorithms in computer vision and NLP, but their computational complexity and high resource demand challenge deployment on CPUs / GPUs. Our lab develops (i) software-based model-compression techniques for energy & memory efficiency and (ii) domain-specific accelerators for both CNNs and transformer models.

Current efforts focus on on-device training (edge computing) and compression of large models, including Vision Transformers (ViT) and LLMs.
